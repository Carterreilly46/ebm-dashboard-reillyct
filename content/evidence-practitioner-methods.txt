**Goal:** Understand HOW executive communication during organizational change affects role clarity and productivity in real-world practice.

## üéØ Acquisition Strategy: Published Case Study Analysis

### Why Case Studies?

We analyzed **published case studies from trusted sources** (McKinsey, Harvard Business Review, GAO) to understand practitioner wisdom about organizational change communication. 

**Case studies provide:**
- ‚úÖ Real organizations, real outcomes (not theory)
- ‚úÖ Practitioner perspectives from executives who led change
- ‚úÖ Implementation details (what worked, what didn't)
- ‚úÖ Measurable results (productivity, engagement, timeline)
- ‚úÖ Lessons learned from experience, not experiments

---

## üìã Selection Criteria

### Source Credibility:
- Published by top-tier consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte)
- Published in peer-reviewed business journals (HBR, Sloan Management Review)
- Government audits with practitioner interviews (GAO reports)

### Relevance to X‚ÜíM‚ÜíY Hypothesis:
- Must involve organizational change (merger, restructuring, culture change)
- Must address executive communication challenges
- Must discuss employee role clarity or confusion during change
- Must measure productivity/performance outcomes
- Must document timeline and process

### Context Similarity:
- Large organizations (1,000+ employees preferred)
- Complex change initiatives (not minor adjustments)
- Multi-level organizations (executives ‚Üí managers ‚Üí employees)
- Knowledge workers (not manufacturing/assembly)

## üìö Primary Case Study Selected

### **McKinsey: European Retail Bank Transformation (2003)**

**Source:** McKinsey Quarterly, "The Psychology of Change Management" by Emily Lawson & Colin Price

**Why This Case?**
- ‚úÖ **Large scale:** 30,000+ employees
- ‚úÖ **Ambitious goals:** Double economic profit, reduce costs 56%‚Üí49%, increase revenue growth 1-2%‚Üí5-7% in 4 years
- ‚úÖ **Culture transformation:** Bureaucracy ‚Üí entrepreneurial decision-making
- ‚úÖ **Communication focus:** CEO used "dialogue-based planning" cascading through 5 organizational levels
- ‚úÖ **Role clarity emphasis:** Every employee had to understand their role in the change story
- ‚úÖ **Measured results:** Halfway to targets after 2 years, 30%+ productivity improvements documented
- ‚úÖ **Published by McKinsey:** Credible, detailed, not self-promotional

**Access:** Publicly available on McKinsey.com, no paywall restrictions

## üîç Data Extraction Method

### What We Documented:

**1. Communication Approach Used:**
- Specific communication strategies executives employed
- Frequency, channels, content of communication
- How communication cascaded through organizational levels
- Role of immediate managers as "communication channels"

**2. Role Clarity Mechanisms:**
- How employees were helped to understand expectations
- Performance scorecards and "deliverable" assignments
- Feedback loops (information flowing up AND down)
- Personal development plans tied to new behaviors

**3. Productivity Outcomes:**
- Quantitative results (cost ratios, revenue growth, profit)
- Qualitative changes (culture shifts, employee behavior)
- Timeline to results (2 years into 4-year program)
- Specific examples (branch queue times ‚Üì30%, flexible shift systems)

**4. Practitioner Wisdom:**
- What executives learned during implementation
- Challenges faced and how overcome
- Would-do-differently insights
- Advice for others attempting similar change

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations We Acknowledged:

- **Single context:** One bank, European market, early 2000s
- **Publication bias:** McKinsey chose to publish a success story
- **Retrospective:** Written after partial success, may have hindsight bias
- **Limited failure detail:** Less information about what went wrong
- **No control group:** Can't isolate which interventions mattered most

## üéì Supporting Case Evidence

### **GAO Federal Agency Reorganizations (2010-2024)**

**Source:** Government Accountability Office audit reports

**What We Extracted:**
- 30+ federal agency reorganizations analyzed
- 75% of failures cited "lack of consistent executive communication"
- Employee quotes about communication breakdowns
- Cost impacts ($3-7 billion aggregate)
- Success factors from agencies that communicated well

**Why This Matters:**
Validates that McKinsey findings generalize beyond private sector to government context (relevant to our FEVS organizational data).

## üìä Analysis Approach

### Cross-Case Pattern Recognition:

**Step 1:** Document McKinsey case in detail (communication ‚Üí clarity ‚Üí outcomes)

**Step 2:** Compare with GAO federal cases (do patterns hold?)

**Step 3:** Identify consensus practitioner wisdom (what do executives consistently recommend?)

**Step 4:** Note divergent perspectives (where do contexts differ?)

**Step 5:** Extract actionable implementation insights (what could we actually do?)
