# Stakeholder Analysis
# Identify and analyze all parties affected by your problem and solution

## Internal Stakeholders

### Frontline Employees
- **Interest in Problem:** Directly experience role confusion and anxiety during organizational changes
- **Interest in Solution:** Need clear, frequent updates to understand their role, job security, and expectations
- **Influence Level:** Medium - Their productivity and engagement directly affect outcomes
- **Support Level:** Strong supporters - desperately want more clarity and communication

### Middle Management
- **Interest in Problem:** Caught between executive decisions and team questions; struggle without clear information
- **Interest in Solution:** Require timely executive communications to effectively lead teams through change
- **Influence Level:** High - Critical translators of executive messages and culture carriers
- **Support Level:** Strong supporters, but concerned about time required for cascading messages

### Senior Leadership/Executives
- **Interest in Problem:** Responsible for successful change execution; reputation at stake
- **Interest in Solution:** Need efficient ways to maintain trust and productivity during transitions
- **Influence Level:** Very High - Decision-makers and resource allocators
- **Support Level:** Mixed - Supporters if time-efficient; may resist increased communication burden

### Human Resources (HR) Department
- **Interest in Problem:** Manage increased turnover, anxiety, and employee relations issues during change
- **Interest in Solution:** Reduces burden of crisis management and retention challenges
- **Influence Level:** High - Partners with leadership on change management strategy
- **Support Level:** Strong supporters - directly benefits their workload and effectiveness

### Internal Communications Team
- **Interest in Problem:** Responsible for message creation and distribution during changes
- **Interest in Solution:** Clear mandate and resources to implement structured communication
- **Influence Level:** Medium-High - Executes communication strategy
- **Support Level:** Very strong supporters - aligns with their core mission

### IT/Technology Teams
- **Interest in Problem:** May need to support communication platforms and tools
- **Interest in Solution:** Requires infrastructure for increased communication channels
- **Influence Level:** Medium - Enables technical execution
- **Support Level:** Neutral to supportive - depends on resource requirements

### Other Internal Groups
**Union Representatives / Employee Resource Groups**
- **Interest in Problem:** Represent employee concerns during organizational uncertainty
- **Interest in Solution:** Want transparent, frequent communication to reduce member anxiety
- **Influence Level:** Medium - Voice of collective employee concerns
- **Support Level:** Strong supporters if communication is genuine and two-way

## External Stakeholders

### Customers/Clients
- **Interest in Problem:** May experience service disruptions if employees are confused or disengaged
- **Interest in Solution:** Benefit from maintained service quality during organizational transitions
- **Influence Level:** High - Revenue impact and brand reputation
- **Support Level:** Indirectly supportive - care about outcomes, not internal processes

### Shareholders/Investors
- **Interest in Problem:** Concerned about productivity losses and failed change initiatives
- **Interest in Solution:** Want successful transitions that protect company value
- **Influence Level:** Very High - Financial stakeholders with board influence
- **Support Level:** Supportive if demonstrates ROI and risk mitigation

### Industry Analysts/Media
- **Interest in Problem:** Report on organizational changes and employee morale
- **Interest in Solution:** Story of successful change management and employee engagement
- **Influence Level:** Medium - Shape public perception
- **Support Level:** Neutral - interested in outcomes and newsworthy stories

### Competitors
- **Interest in Problem:** May benefit from our talent leaving during poorly managed change
- **Interest in Solution:** We become harder to compete against if we retain talent
- **Influence Level:** Low - No direct influence on our decisions
- **Support Level:** Not applicable - external observers

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

### High Influence, High Interest (Manage Closely)
- Senior Leadership: One-on-one briefings, involvement in communication strategy design
- Middle Management: Weekly planning sessions, early preview of messages, feedback loops
- HR Department: Joint planning, co-ownership of change management approach
- Shareholders/Investors: Quarterly updates on change progress and employee engagement metrics

### High Influence, Low Interest (Keep Satisfied)
- IT Department: Technical requirements briefings, adequate lead time for infrastructure
- Board of Directors: High-level summaries of communication effectiveness metrics
- Legal/Compliance: Review of communication content for risk management
- Finance Department: Budget justifications and ROI projections

### Low Influence, High Interest (Keep Informed)
- Frontline Employees: Regular town halls, email updates, accessible Q&A channels
- Internal Communications Team: Daily coordination, content development collaboration
- Union Representatives: Transparent sharing of communication plans and timelines
- Employee Resource Groups: Regular briefings and feedback opportunities

### Low Influence, Low Interest (Monitor)
- Industry Media: Press releases on major milestones only
- Competitors: No direct engagement
- General Public: Standard corporate communications
- Industry Associations: Periodic case study sharing (post-implementation)

## Key Stakeholder Insights for Evidence Collection

### Who to Interview/Survey
- Employees at various levels who experienced recent organizational changes
- Middle managers who cascade executive messages to teams
- HR business partners supporting change initiatives
- Internal communications professionals with change experience
- Executives who have led major organizational transitions
- Employee representatives (union reps, ERG leaders)

### What Questions to Ask
- How often do you currently receive updates from leadership during changes?
- How clear are you about your role and responsibilities during transitions?
- What communication methods are most effective for you (email, video, town halls)?
- How does communication frequency affect your productivity and anxiety levels?
- What would ideal executive communication look like during organizational change?
- How quickly do you need information to maintain productivity?

### How to Engage
- Employees: Anonymous online surveys, focus groups by department/level
- Middle Management: In-depth interviews and workshops
- Senior Leadership: One-on-one executive interviews
- HR Teams: Collaborative data analysis sessions
- Past Change Participants: Retrospective surveys and case studies
- Internal Communications: Process mapping and capability assessment
