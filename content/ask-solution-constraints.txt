## üöß Resource Constraints
### Budget Limitations
- Communication platform costs must fit within existing IT/HR budgets
- Video production and town hall logistics require modest investment
- Survey tools and analytics software may need new licenses
- ROI expected through reduced turnover costs and maintained productivity

### Time Constraints
- Executive schedules are heavily committed during transitions
- Communication prep time competes with other change management tasks
- Cannot delay critical business decisions to produce communications
- Messages must be timely (within 24-48 hours of major decisions)

### Human Resource Constraints
- Limited internal communications team capacity (2-3 FTE)
- Executive availability for regular video recordings and town halls
- Manager time needed to cascade messages to teams
- HR bandwidth to monitor employee sentiment and feedback

### Technical Constraints
- Must work with existing email, intranet, and video platforms
- Mobile accessibility for remote/field employees
- Accessibility compliance (captions, transcripts) required
- Integration with existing HR/engagement survey tools
- Data privacy for employee feedback and sentiment tracking

## üë• Organizational Constraints
### Policy Requirements
- Legal review required for sensitive communications (layoffs, restructuring)
- Consistency with employer brand and communication guidelines
- Compliance with labor laws regarding organizational changes
- Union notification requirements (if applicable)
- Securities regulations for publicly traded companies

### Cultural Considerations
- Varying preferences for communication styles across generations
- Different information needs at various organizational levels
- Multiple languages for global workforce
- Remote vs. in-office employee access needs
- Trust levels with leadership vary across departments

### Leadership Limitations
- Executive comfort level with video/public speaking
- Consistency in tone and message across leadership team
- Balancing transparency with confidentiality requirements
- Managing competing demands on executive time
- Varying communication skills among senior leaders

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Constraints
### Communication Risks
- Message fatigue if frequency is too high without value
- Inconsistent messaging across channels or leaders
- Information leaks before official announcements
- Misinterpretation of incomplete information
- Over-promising and under-delivering on commitments

### Operational Risks
- Communication activities cannot disrupt critical operations
- Must maintain business continuity during transitions
- Risk of productivity loss if employees spend too much time in meetings
- Potential for negative sentiment if communications are perceived as insincere
- Backlash if employee feedback is collected but not acted upon

### Change Management Risks
- Resistance from executives who prefer less transparency
- Middle manager anxiety about answering difficult questions
- Employee skepticism based on past communication failures
- Information overload during already stressful transitions
- Unrealistic employee expectations for frequency of updates

## üìä Performance Constraints
### Communication Quality Standards
- Messages must be clear, concise, and action-oriented
- Tone must balance optimism with realism
- Content accuracy verified before distribution
- Consistency across all communication channels
- Professional production quality for video content

### Timeliness Requirements
- Critical updates within 24 hours of decisions
- Regular cadence maintained (2-3 times per week minimum)
- Q&A responses within 48 hours
- Town halls scheduled with adequate notice (5-7 days)
- Survey results analyzed and shared within 1 week

### Measurement Standards
- Role clarity surveys must achieve 70%+ response rate
- Productivity metrics tracked weekly with 95% accuracy
- Employee sentiment tracked with validated tools
- Communication reach: 90%+ of employees access messages
- Engagement metrics: 60%+ open/view rates for communications

### Scalability Needs
- System must support organization size (e.g., 5,000-50,000 employees)
- Scalable to multiple business units and geographies
- Adaptable communication frequency based on change intensity
- Sustainable long-term without burning out communicators
- Replicable framework for future organizational changes

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Consider all constraint categories when designing communication strategy
2. Prioritize constraints based on organizational impact and feasibility
3. Document assumptions and dependencies for executive buy-in
4. Update constraints as implementation reveals new challenges
5. Balance ideal frequency with realistic executive and employee capacity
